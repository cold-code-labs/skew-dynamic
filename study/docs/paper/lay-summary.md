When you bet on the favourite you usually win a little; when you bet on the underdog
you usually lose, but occasionally win big. This lopsidedness — statisticians call it
skewness — is often blamed on bettors who love long-shot gambles. We show it is
something simpler: a mathematical shadow of how evenly matched the teams are. Across
205,435 football matches over twenty years it never drifts, and the same pattern
appears in tennis and basketball. The asymmetry is built into the sport's competitive
structure, not created by the betting market, which merely reflects it.
